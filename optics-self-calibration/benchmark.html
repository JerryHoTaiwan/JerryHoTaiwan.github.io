Classical deconvolution benchmark

The classical benchmark was supposed to provide an upper bound by giving each method the exact GT PSF. Instead it demonstrates why the image prior is necessary.

The Fourier-domain reason is straightforward: the optical PSF is a severe low-pass filter, so classical inversion divides by very small values exactly where the measurement SNR is worst. That instability is structural, not a tuning issue.

Open questions and next directions

  • Can a better image diffusion model raise the +3.75 dB ceiling?
  • Can an optical PSF prior be made useful without triggering the feedback loop?
  • What changes when the PSF is spatially varying?
  • How well do these conclusions transfer to measured real images?

The main unresolved scientific issue is now clear: once the kernel is good enough, image modeling quality becomes the dominant bottleneck.
